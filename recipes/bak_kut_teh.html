Bak Kut Teh

Description

Bak kut teh is a popular dish in Southeast Asia, particularly among the Chinese community. It consists of pork ribs simmered in a flavorful broth infused with herbs and spices such as garlic, star anise, and peppercorns.

There are two main variations: herbal and peppery. It's traditionally served with steamed rice or youtiao (Chinese fried dough). The dish is loved for its rich, savory broth and tender pork ribs.

Ingredients

  • Pork ribs
  • Garlic
  • Soy sauce
  • White pepper
  • Chinese herbs
  • water

Steps

  1. In a large pot, combine the pork ribs, minced garlic, soy sauce, white pepper, and water. If using bak kut teh spice mix, add it to the pot as well.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer gently for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until the pork ribs are tender and the flavors have melded together. Skim off any foam or impurities that rise to the surface during simmering.
  3. Taste the broth and adjust the seasoning with salt and sugar if needed, according to your preference.
  4. Once the pork ribs are tender and the broth is flavorful, remove the pot from the heat.
  5. Serve the bak kut teh hot, garnished with chopped green onions and fried shallots if desired. It's traditionally enjoyed with steamed rice or youtiao (Chinese fried dough) on the side.
